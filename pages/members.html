NAYEON

Im Na-Yeon (Hangul: 임나연) was born on September 22, 1995, in Seoul, South Korea. She is the oldest member, lead vocalist, and face of the girl group TWICE.

Nayeon secretly tried out and passed JYP Entertainment's 7th Open Audition and became a trainee on September 15, 2010. Prior to Sixteen and her debut as a member of TWICE, she appeared in a few television advertisements and in several music videos of her label mates, including Jun. K's "No Love", Got7's "Girls Girls Girls" and miss A's "Only You".

Nayeon debuted as a member of TWICE on October 20, 2015, with the release of their debut EP The Story Begins. Nayeon made her solo debut on June 24, 2022 with her first mini album "IM NAYEON" with the tittle track "POP!"

JEONGYEON

Jeongyeon (Hangul: 정연) is a South Korean singer under JYP Entertainment. She is a member of the girl group TWICE. On May 5, 2015, she became a contestant on JYP's newest survival show, SIXTEEN. She finished the survival in 3rd place, hence becoming a member of the girl group TWICE. They officially debuted on October 20, 2015, with their first mini album, The Story Begins.

On June 22, 2016, SBS revealed that Jeongyeon, alongside her sister Gong Seung Yeon and actor Kim Min Su, would be the new MCs for the music show Inkigayo. She worked on the music show until January 22.

On 17 October 2020, JYP Entertainment announced that Jeongyeon would be taking a break from all activities due to anxiety.

MOMO

Momo Hirai (平井 もも), popularly known as Momo (Hangul: 모모), was born on November 9, 1996, in Kyōtanabe, Kyoto, Japan. She is best known as the main dancer of the girl group TWICE under JYP Entertainment.

Momo started dancing at a very young age. Her talent and passion lead to her to train at 'Step Out Studio' in Japan. There, she was scouted by a JYP Entertainment agent and was invited to audition for the 2011 JYP Japan Audition. Momo passed and ended up moving to South Korea as an official JYP Entertainment trainee.

Years later, in 2015, Momo appeared as a contestant in the the survival show SIXTEEN. Despite getting eliminated, Momo was brought back by JYP and ended up debuting as a member of TWICE.

SANA

Sana Minatozaki (湊崎 紗夏), known mononymously as Sana (Hangul:사나), was born on December 29, 1996, in Tennōji-Ku, Osaka, Japan.

Sana attended EXPG (Exile Professional Gym) Osaka branch, dance and vocal studio from 2009 to early 2012. In her last year of middle school, she was scouted by JYP Entertainment on the street and auditioned on April 13, 2012.

In 2015, Sana participated in the music survival show Sixteen, a reality television series designed to select the final members of TWICE. In February 2021, Sana became the first Twice member to release a single after she made a cover of "Sotsugyou", a 2020 single by Japanese band Kobukuro.

JIHYO

Park Jisoo (Hangul: 박지수), popularly known as Jihyo, was born on February 1, 1997, in Guri, Gyeonggi-do, South Korea. She rose to fame as the leader and main vocalist of the girl group TWICE under JYP Entertainment.

When Jihyo was in third grade, she participated in a contest on Junior Naver for a child role. After she won second place, she was scouted by JYP Entertainment. She officially joined the company on July 15, 2005, and was a trainee for over 10 years.

In 2015, Jihyo appeared as a contestant on the show SIXTEEN where she ranked 5th place, allowing her to debut as a member of TWICE. Jihyo debuted as a songwriter on TWICE's new album 'Formula of Love'

MINA

Mina Myoui (名井 南 Myōi Mina), also known as Mina (Hangul: 미나), was born on March 24, 1997, in San Antonio, Texas, and moved to Kobe, Japan when she was a toddler. She rose to fame after debuting as one of the members of the sensational girl group TWICE.

In 2013, Mina was scouted by a JYP Entertainment staff while shopping with her mother. She passed the JYP Japan Audition and moved to South Korea where she began her journey as an official idol trainee.

After two years, she participated in the survival show SIXTEEN where she ranked fourth place, securing her spot in the group.

DAHYUN

Dahyun (Hangul: 다현) is a South Korean singer under JYP Entertainment. She is popularly known as a member of the sensational girl group TWICE.

Before making her official debut, Dahyun used to be a member of their church's choir group. Besides performing in church, she also used to participate in dance festivals which was where she was scouted by a JYP Entertainment staff. After the passing the audtion, she became an official trainee.

In 2015, she participated in the survival show SIXTEEN where she became one of the winners, securing her spot in the group TWICE. They officially debuted on October 20, 2015, with their first mini album, The Story Begins.

CHAEYOUNG

Chaeyoung (Hangul: 챼영) is a South Korean rapper, singer, songwriter and designer under JYP Entertainment. She is the main rapper of the girl group TWICE.

On May 5, 2015, she became a contestant on JYP's survival show, SIXTEEN. She ended up becoming one of the members, hence securing her spot in TWICE. They officially debuted on October 20, 2015, with their first mini-album, The Story Begins.

Chaeyoung became the first member to receive writing credits when she wrote the rap verse for Twice's version of J.Y Park's Precious Love, as part of their 2016 release Page Two. Moreover, Chaeyoung designed the group's limited edition Page Two album cover and also designed three different pairs of SPRIS shoes.

TZUYU

Tzuyu (Hangul: 쯔위) is a Taiwanese singer-songwriter under JYP Entertainment. She is the maknae of the girl group TWICE. After more than two years of training, she appeared on the South Korean reality show Sixteen in 2015, during which she was chosen as one of the nine members of the girl group TWICE.

In November 2015, Tzuyu appeared with Twice on the Korean variety show My Little Television. She introduced herself as Taiwanese and held the flag of Republic of China (Taiwan) alongside that of South Korea. Mainland Chinese internet users reacted angrily towards Tzuyu's actions due to the political rift between the two countries.

That's why her agency, JYP Entertainment, released a video showing Tzuyu reading an apology.
